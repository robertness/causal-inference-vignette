New Mindmap

  • We next simulate an experiment to elucidate how dimensionality affects causal network inference

    We simulate data from a dependence network of 60 proteins and evaluate performance of structure inference algorithms in recovering the structure.

    We then simulate a second experiment where the number of proteins is increased 99, to see how the increased protein coverage affected performance.

    We varied depth as well as breadth -- In order to evaluate the effect of increasing sample size we start with 100 replicates, then repeat the two experiments with 200 replicates.

    We evaluate performance by counting the total number of false positives (reject conditional independence for two conditionally independent proteins) and true positives (correct rejections)

    We built the dependence structure using the simple case of variable triplets

    • Testing conditional independence requires at least three variables -- two to compare, and at least one variable upon which to condition.

      Thus we created triplets of Guassian variables A, B, and C

      • C has a normal distribution with 0 mean and variance 1

        A and B were also created as 0 mean Gaussian variables with individual variance of 1 and covariance with C of .6

        A and B are conditionally independent of eachother given C.

        Thus for a triplet there are two edges, one from A to C and B to C, while the edge from A to B is missing.

      Thus we created a network of 60 proteins with 20 triplets -- disjoint subnetworks with two edges each, 40 in total.

    We examined two cases for expanding the number of measured proteins

    • The first describes the case of measuring proteins that provide no additional information about the biology.

      We model this scenariao by  adding 39 independently simulated proteins in the second experiment  --  they are completely independent from anything else in the network such that concluding that an edge contained one of these nodes would be a false positive.

      We then look at the case where more proteins are added, but the added proteins are informative about the biology.

      We model this by increasing the number of triplets from 20 to 33, again to 99 proteins in total.
